# Audience Profiles

**Brand:** Malt & Barley Public House
**Last updated:** 2026-04-22
**Source:** Brand Interview Sections B + F + Research Output Sections 5 and 6

---

## Segment 1: The Williamsburg Regular

**Who they are:**
Lives or works within a short drive of Fischer-Hallman. Has been to Malt & Barley before and has a usual order. Treats this pub the way people treat a good barbershop - habitual, trusted, low-friction. Knows about trivia Tuesday and which nights have live music. May or may not have discovered the monthly chef specials yet.

**What brings them here:**
Routine. A Tuesday night out. Wing Wednesday because it's $13 and reliably good. A Saturday with a game on. They don't need a reason - they need the pub to be consistent.

**What they search for:**
Rarely searching - they already know about the place. When they do search it's: "malt and barley hours," "malt and barley kitchener," "what's on at malt and barley." Navigation and confirmation intent.

**What earns their trust:**
Content that confirms what they already know and occasionally surfaces something new (a seasonal special, a new tap, an upcoming event). Not being talked to like a newcomer.

**What loses them:**
Over-explanation of basics. Content that reads like it was written for someone who has never heard of the place. Hype language that doesn't match the actual experience.

**Content priority for this segment:** [x] Primary

## Segment 2: The East Coast Transplant

**Who they are:**
Grew up in Nova Scotia, New Brunswick, PEI, or Newfoundland. Now lives somewhere in Kitchener-Waterloo Region. Misses the food - specifically the donair, the fish and chips, the particular comfort of a pub that actually knows what a donair is. Has probably searched for "Halifax donair Kitchener" at some point.

**What brings them here:**
Recognition. The donair is made on a vertical spit with the right sauce. That's not nothing. For this segment, Malt & Barley isn't just a pub - it's the closest thing to home food they've found since moving here.

**What they search for:**
"Halifax donair Kitchener," "East Coast food Kitchener," "Maritime food KW," "donair Waterloo Region." High intent, specific. They know what they want - they're searching to see if it exists nearby.

**What earns their trust:**
Specificity. A blog post that correctly explains what a Halifax donair actually is (and what it is not) signals immediately that the kitchen knows what it's doing. The details matter to this segment more than any other.

**What loses them:**
Getting the donair wrong in print. Describing it generically. Anything that suggests the East Coast identity is just a marketing angle rather than a real commitment.

**Content priority for this segment:** [x] Primary

## Segment 3: The Kitchener-Area Pub Seeker

**Who they are:**
Lives anywhere in Kitchener-Waterloo Region. Not necessarily local to Williamsburg. Looking for a pub for a specific occasion - a group outing, a sports night, a place with good food that isn't a chain. Has probably googled "best pubs Kitchener" or "pub with good food Kitchener" at some point.

**What brings them here:**
Discovery. This segment doesn't know Malt & Barley yet, or knows the name but hasn't been. They're comparison-shopping pubs for an occasion and need a reason to choose this one over the five others in the search results.

**What they search for:**
"Best pub Kitchener," "pub food Kitchener," "Kitchener sports bar," "fish and chips Kitchener," "pub with live music Kitchener." Exploratory intent. Higher volume than Segment 2, lower conversion unless the content is specific enough to close.

**What earns their trust:**
A blog that answers their exact question better than any competitor. Concrete details - what's actually on the menu, what events actually happen, what the experience actually feels like. Social proof (reviews, press mentions).

**What loses them:**
Generic content that reads like every other pub in the city. Anything that makes the place sound like a chain or a tourist destination.

**Content priority for this segment:** [x] Secondary

## Segment 4: The Family and Groups Planner

**Who they are:**
Planning a dinner for a group - family night out, birthday gathering, work drinks, post-game meal. Needs a pub that works for a mixed crowd: adults who want beer and food, possibly kids, possibly someone who doesn't drink. Searching with logistics in mind.

**What brings them here:**
Practicality + quality. Kids menu, group-friendly layout, private event option, accessible location. The fact that the food is actually good seals it.

**What they search for:**
"Family-friendly pub Kitchener," "pub for groups Kitchener," "private dining Kitchener," "kid-friendly restaurant Kitchener." Also: "pub near Williamsburg" for those already in the area.

**What earns their trust:**
Explicit confirmation that the experience works for groups - mentions of the kids menu, the layout, the events calendar. Practical information delivered clearly.

**What loses them:**
Feeling like the pub is only for hardcore regulars. Anything that suggests kids or groups aren't really welcome.

**Content priority for this segment:** [x] Secondary

---

## Primary Segment for Content Default

When content is not targeted at a specific segment, it is written for: **The Williamsburg Regular** - assume the reader already knows the place and is looking to deepen the relationship, not be introduced to it.
