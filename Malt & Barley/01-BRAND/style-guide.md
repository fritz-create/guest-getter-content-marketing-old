# Writing Style Guide

**Brand:** Malt & Barley Public House
**Last updated:** 2026-04-22
**Source:** Brand Interview + Voice Document

---

## Point of View

- **Blog posts:** First person plural ("we") for restaurant perspective; second person ("you") when addressing the reader directly. Mix naturally - avoid locking into one for the entire piece.
- **GBP posts:** First person plural ("we") or direct address ("you") - keep it conversational.
- **Avoid:** Switching POV mid-paragraph. Third person ("Malt & Barley is a place where...") only for SEO-driven introductions where needed.

---

## Sentence & Paragraph Structure

- **Preferred sentence length:** Varied - short sentences for impact, longer ones for context. Average under 20 words.
- **Max paragraph length:** 3-4 lines on screen / 3 sentences max for most body paragraphs
- **One-sentence paragraphs:** Allowed and encouraged for emphasis
- **Opening sentences:** Never start with "Welcome to" or the restaurant name. Open with the subject matter, a scene, or a question.

---

## Formatting Rules

- **Headers:** Sentence case (not Title Case)
- **Numbers:** Spell out one through nine, numerals for 10 and above. Always use numerals for prices, hours, and quantities.
- **Dates:** Month D, YYYY (e.g., April 7, 2019). Day of week spelled out.
- **Oxford comma:** Yes - always
- **Em dash:** Never. Use a hyphen (-) surrounded by spaces where a pause is needed, or restructure the sentence.
- **Exclamation marks:** Maximum one per piece. Avoid entirely in blog posts. Acceptable in GBP posts sparingly.
- **Ellipses:** Avoid in formal content. Acceptable in very casual GBP posts only.

---

## Reading Level

- **Target reading level:** 7th-8th grade
- **Jargon policy:** Use pub and food terms naturally (draught, pint, schnitzel) - the audience knows them. Define regional terms when introducing them (e.g., explain what a Halifax donair is the first time it appears in a post).

---

## CTAs and Action Language

- **CTA style:** Soft invite or direct - context dependent. "Come in Wednesday." / "Book a table." / "See what's on tap this week."
- **How reservations are referenced:** "Reserve a table" or "book ahead" - not "make a reservation" (too formal)
- **How ordering online is referenced:** "Order online" or "get it delivered" - plain and direct
- **Where to put CTAs:** End of GBP posts always. End of blog posts - one only, low-pressure.

---

## What We Never Write

- We never use competitor names in content
- We never make unsubstantiated superlative claims ("best pub in Kitchener" unless awarded/verified)
- We never make health or dietary claims without confirmation from the kitchen
- We never fabricate statistics - every number cited needs a source URL or it gets removed
- We never write in a voice that sounds like it came from a content agency in Toronto

---

## Blog Post Formatting

- **Length:** 800-1,500 words for most posts. Long-form (1,500+) only when the topic warrants depth.
- **H1:** One per post - the title. Sentence case.
- **H2s:** 2-4 per post. Each one moves the piece forward - not decorative.
- **H3s:** Used for sub-sections within an H2 when needed. Not required.
- **Images:** At least one per post. Alt text required. Descriptive, not keyword-stuffed.
- **Internal links:** At least one per post (once other posts exist to link to).
- **External links:** Source citations only - research, press coverage, or reference material. Never link to competitors.

---

## GBP Post Formatting

- **Max length:** 300 words. Target 100-200.
- **Structure:** Hook (what's happening) - why it matters - soft CTA. Three beats, that's it.
- **No hashtags** in GBP posts
- **Every GBP post must be tied to a source blog post** once the blog is active (Phase 6 rule)
