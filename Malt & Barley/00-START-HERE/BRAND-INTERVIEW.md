## SECTION A - The Brand

**A1. [R] What does this restaurant serve? How would you describe it to a stranger?**
> Celtic-inspired pub in Kitchener's Williamsburg neighbourhood. East Coast-style comfort food - scratch-made dishes like donairs, fish & chips, pot roast, sticky toffee pudding. 24 rotating draught taps with local craft beer. Mid-range pricing with daily $13 specials Mon-Thu. Live music, trivia, sports on 8 TVs. (from research - confirm or override)

**A2. [C] How do you want people to feel when they walk in or open your menu?**
> Like they already belong here. The kind of place locals know about and feel some ownership over - not a discovery, a given. The pub the neighbourhood would miss if it were gone. (from user input + brand signals)

**A3. [C] What's the one thing you do better than anyone else in your area?**
> The food - specifically scratch-made, traditionally prepared East Coast comfort food that nobody else in Kitchener is doing. The donair is the clearest proof point: made on a vertical spit, kept as traditional as possible, and it shows. (inferred from owner quote and research - confirm or override)

**A4. [E] What's the origin story, if any? How did this place come to exist?**
> Opened 2019. Third restaurant for Sabrina and David (Dave) Hutchison - they also own Scran and Dram Scottish Public House (338 Waterloo St, New Hamburg) and Imperial Market and Eatery (New Hamburg). Dave is co-owner and chef, originally from Scotland. Business partner Mike is formerly a manager at Scran and Dram. Dave visited Halifax, experienced the iconic donair after a night out, and decided to bring Maritime comfort food culture to landlocked Waterloo Region - acquiring a vertical spit and building the concept around it. Direct quotes: "We've kept the donair as traditional as possible. And we sell a lot of them." / "The donair sauce is condensed milk, some sugar and some garlic powder. It's sweet and garlicky, and it's quite messy." Location is Williamsburg Town Centre specifically. (from research - confirmed via multiple sources)

**A5. [C] What do you wish more people knew about you?**
> That the kitchen is fully scratch-made - this isn't reheated pub fare. The food is taken seriously. And the donair is as authentic as you'll find 1,800km from Halifax. (inferred from research: TripAdvisor praises fresh ingredients and quality; Coppolino article emphasizes authenticity - confirm or override)

## Interview Summary

**Brand identity and positioning:**
Malt & Barley Public House is a scratch-kitchen pub that brought East Coast Maritime food culture to Kitchener's Williamsburg neighbourhood. Opened in 2019 by Dave and Sabrina Hutchison (Scottish owners who also run Scran and Dram and Imperial Market in New Hamburg), the pub was built around an authentic Halifax donair and the kind of comfort food that doesn't exist elsewhere in the KW region. Above all, it is a local institution in the truest sense - the kind of place the neighbourhood knows about, talks about, and feels ownership over. Not a destination restaurant, not a chain, not a brand trying to be found - a pub that locals have already claimed as theirs. Events (trivia, music bingo, live music, sports) make it a weekly habit, not an occasional outing.

**Primary audience:**
Williamsburg locals who want a reliable neighbourhood pub; East Coast transplants across KW who recognize the food; sports fans; families who need a pub that actually works for kids; after-work groups.

**Voice in plain language:**
Warm, unpretentious, genuine. Not trying to impress - trying to welcome. The kind of voice that says "we made this from scratch" without making it a whole thing.

**Top content goal:**
Drive discovery. The restaurant is neighbourhood-located without walk-by traffic. Search visibility is the gap - and the competitive space is wide open (zero competitors have functioning blogs).
